Team Tiny House

Mission

  • We believe in a sustainable future for the planet
  • We will design and construct a sustainable tiny home and educate our community about sustainable living
  • Team Tiny House is not just building a house, we are engineering a future!

WHY

Vision:

Our team consists of 40 Thomas Jefferson High School students who dream of asustainable future for the planet.

HOW

Strategy:

We research, design, and construct a tiny house on wheels that balances sustainability and affordability. Afterwards, we will showcase it throughout Northern Virginia (at science centers, festivals, etc.) and provide resources to inspire sustainable living.

WHAT

Outcome:

Our outcome is to see our community be educated about sustainable living. Additionally, we want our community to adopt environmentally friendly housing practices.

Target Audience

Our fully functioning tiny house will be located in one place for ¾ of the year, open as a walk-in exhibit for families and children. The other ¼ of the year our tiny house will be moved throughout the community - visiting schools, festivals, and events.

Our Tiny House Model

Our Approach

1

Design

Spring 2022 We will conduct extensive research to design the most environmentally friendly house with the latest technology, at reasonable cost. We make careful decisions to prioritize sustainability and affordability. Each sub-team conducts research in their field, and we all collaborate during after school meetings to ensure our plans are compatible.

2

Tinker

Summer-Winter 2022 Over the summer, we will begin to experiment and innovate in our sub-teams to learn the ropes of making tiny houses. Our plans include securing funding for the school year, testing electronic equipment with loose wiring, cardboard structural models, and refurbishing cheap facebook marketplace furniture. In the fall and through the winter, we will start building small sections of the house; we will build all the parts of the house separately, making sure they work together, indoors while the weather is too cold. Having loose pieces allows us to store compactly indoors and prototype faster while we’re still learning.

3

Engineer

Spring-Summer 2023 Next Spring, we will combine all the pieces constructed in the “tinker” phase on a full-sized tiny house. As soon as the weather warms up, we will start final framing, roofing, installing electrical wires, and installing plumbing. Afterwards, we will add the interior elements and add finishing touches.

4

Document

Fall 2023 Our long term goal is to make detailed tiny house guides for others to easily build an affordable home. As we’re building, we plan to post updates and our immediate findings to our website, but this will be unplanned and mainly for our reference. Towards the end of tiny house construction, we will make official TTH guides for the general public to easily follow. The guides will most likely be a combination of written findings and video tutorials. Additionally, the guides will be made open free to the public.
